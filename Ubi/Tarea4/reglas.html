Bike Race Safety Rules

Mountain biking is an inherently risky activity. These Safety Rules are not a guarantee against injury or death to riders, spectators, or others. However, you can significantly reduce your risk by riding with skill and awareness, using high-quality protective gear, maintaining your equipment properly, and reporting unsafe conditions or behaviour to race officials.

Riders are solely responsible for ensuring that their bicycles are fit for competition. Meeting minimum safety standards does not equal optimal protection.

1. Rider Responsibility

  • Inspect equipment before each race:
    • Proper brake function (front and rear)
    • Securely fastened wheels
    • Intact drivetrain and suspension components
  • Be self-reliant:
    • Carry basic repair tools
    • Know basic first aid procedures
  • Respect all race protocols and briefings

2. Equipment & Safety Gear

2.1 Helmets

  • Must meet certifications: CPSC, EN1078, ASTM F1952
  • Must fit snugly and be worn at all times

2.2 Protective Gear

  • Gloves, eye protection, knee and elbow pads
  • Full-face helmets required for Enduro and Downhill races

2.3 Bike Standards

  • Bikes must be appropriate for terrain and class
  • No motorized assistance unless explicitly allowed

3. Course Conduct

3.1 On-Course Behavior

  • Stay within marked trail boundaries
  • Yield to emergency personnel
  • No aggressive or unsafe behavior

3.2 Passing Protocol

  • Verbally announce passes
  • Lapped riders must yield safely

4. Weather & Environmental Conditions

  • Race Director may delay or cancel events
  • Riders must prepare for extreme weather

5. Accidents, Crashes & Injuries

  • Stop and notify officials if a rider is injured
  • Medical clearance required after serious injuries

6. Prohibited Substances & Behavior

  • Zero tolerance for alcohol and drugs
  • Disqualification for abusive or destructive behavior

7. Authority of Race Officials

Race officials may issue penalties, disqualify riders, or remove unsafe participants. All decisions are final.

8. Final Acknowledgment

By registering, riders confirm they have read, understood, and agree to comply with all safety regulations.
